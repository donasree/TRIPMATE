Andaman and Nicobar Islands

The Andaman and Nicobar Islands, a union territory of India, comprise over 800 islands situated at the southeastern edge of the Bay of Bengal. These islands are renowned for their pristine beaches, lush forests, and rich biodiversity, making them a captivating destination for travelers seeking natural beauty and tranquility.‚Äã These islands offer a blend of historical significance, natural wonders, and adventure activities, making them a must-visit destination for travelers exploring India's diverse landscapes.

Situated in Port Blair, the capital city, this colonial-era prison, also known as "Kala Pani," once incarcerated Indian freedom fighters. Today, it stands as a national memorial, offering visitors a glimpse into India's struggle for independence.

Mahatma Gandhi Marine Park

üìç Andaman & Nicobar Islands, India

Located near Wandoor, this park encompasses a group of islands and is renowned for its rich marine life, including vibrant coral reefs and nesting sea turtles. Islands like Jolly Buoy and Red Skin within the park are popular spots for snorkeling and glass-bottom boat rides.

Ross Island

üìç Andaman & Nicobar Islands, India

Once the administrative headquarters during British rule, this island near Port Blair now features ruins of colonial structures amidst lush greenery, offering a historical and scenic experience.

Baratang Island

üìç Andaman & Nicobar Islands, India

Famous for its limestone caves and mud volcanoes, Baratang provides a unique adventure. Accessible through dense mangrove creeks, the journey itself is an experience to cherish.

Havelock Island

üìç Andaman & Nicobar Islands, India

Beyond Radhanagar Beach, Havelock is home to other beautiful beaches like Elephant Beach and Kalapathar Beach, offering opportunities for snorkeling, scuba diving, and witnessing bioluminescent waters during certain seasons.
